Marina Bay Sands (MBS)


Marina Bay Sands is an integrated resort fronting Marina Bay in Singapore, owned by the Las Vegas Sands Corporation. At its opening in 2010, it was billed as the world's most expensive standalone casino property at S$8 billion, including the land cost.

Features

The resort includes a 2,561-room hotel, a 120,000-square-metre convention-exhibition centre, the The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands mall, a museum, a large theatre, "celebrity chef" restaurants, two floating crystal pavilions, art-science exhibits, and the world's largest atrium casino with 500 tables and 1,600 slot machines.

The complex includes three towers topped by a connecting 340-metre-long SkyPark with a capacity of 3,902 people and a 150 m infinity swimming pool, set on top of the world's largest public cantilevered platform, which overhangs the north tower by 66.5m.

Activities In MBS - Shopping

The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands is a massive shopping mall that scales up to 74,000 square metres. There are a wide variety of things in The Shoppes, such as many different shopping outlets, plenty of entertainment to enjoy, and many delicious food and beverage stores. Being a walk away from MBS makes the Shoppes a very accessible place to shop for almost anything.

Visit https://www.marinabaysands.com/shopping.html for more information.

Activities In MBS - Sightseeing

Gardens by the Bay is one of Singapore's most popular attractions. It was part of the nation's plans to transform its "Garden City" to a "City in a Garden", with the aim of raising the quality of life by enhancing greenery and flora in the city. With numerous exotic plants and landforms that are both man-made and natural, the Gardens by the Bay is a refreshing experience of nature and its wonders. Being only a 10-minute walk away from MBS, Gardens by the Bay is easily accessible from MBS, and is a must to experience.
