Singapore ArtScience Museum

About the ArtScience Museum

Opened on 17 February 2011 by Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong, it is the world's first ArtScience museum, featuring major exhibitions that blend art, science, culture and technology. is an iconic cultural landmark in Singapore.

Our mission is to explore where art, science, culture and technology come together. It is here at the intersection of art and science that innovation and new ideas are formed.

The ArtScience Museum has 21 gallery spaces with a total area of 64,583 square feet (6,000 square meters).

It has held large-scale exhibitions by some of the world's best known artists including Leonardo da Vinci, Salvador Dali, Andy Warhol, Vincent van Gogh and M.C. Escher. It has also presented significnat exhbitions that explore aspects of science including big data, particle physics, paleontology, marine biology, cosmology and space exploration.
